Definitions

Fractals

Fractals are complex geometric shapes or patterns that exhibit self-similarity, meaning they contain smaller copies of themselves at various scales. These intricate structures are generated through recursive mathematical equations and are often characterized by their fine, detailed, and irregular features. Fractals have applications in various fields, including mathematics, art, computer graphics, and natural phenomena modeling.

Color Schemes

Color schemes, also known as color palettes or color harmonies, refer to organized combinations of colors chosen for design or artistic purposes. These schemes are created to achieve visual harmony and convey specific emotions or messages. Common color schemes include monochromatic (using variations of a single color), complementary (combining colors opposite each other on the color wheel), analogous (using colors adjacent to each other on the color wheel), and triadic (selecting three evenly spaced colors on the color wheel), among others. Effective use of color schemes is essential in graphic design, interior decorating, and various forms of visual communication.

Shape Transformation

Shape transformation, in the context of mathematics and computer graphics, involves altering the size, position, orientation, or deformation of geometric shapes. This process is typically achieved through a series of mathematical operations or transformations, such as translation (moving a shape to a different location), rotation (changing the orientation of a shape), scaling (resizing a shape), and shear (distorting a shape by stretching or tilting). Shape transformation is fundamental in computer-aided design (CAD), animation, and computer graphics to create dynamic and visually appealing images or models. It plays a crucial role in simulating real-world movements and transformations in digital environments.
