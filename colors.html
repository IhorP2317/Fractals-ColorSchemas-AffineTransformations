HSL
HSL stands for Hue, Saturation, and Lightness, and it's a color model used in design and image editing. Here's what each component does:
  1. Hue (H): This determines the color itself, ranging from 0 to 360 degrees around a color wheel (e.g., 0 is red, 120 is green, 240 is blue). Changing hue alters the type of color.
  2. Saturation (S): This controls the intensity of the color, from 0% (grayscale) to 100% (fully vibrant). Adjusting saturation makes colors more or less vivid.
  3. Lightness (L): Lightness affects how bright or dark the color appears, ranging from 0% (black) to 100% (white). It doesn't change the color but alters its brightness.
CMYK
CMYK is a color model used in printing. It consists of four ink colors: Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Key (Black). By combining these colors in varying amounts, it produces a wide range of printed colors. CMYK is commonly used in the printing industry for producing magazines, posters, and other printed materials.
